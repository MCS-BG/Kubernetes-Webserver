# Single-Workflow Pilot Playbook

The front door into the methodology in Docs 01-06 — deliberately smaller than
a full Process Audit. One business. One workflow. One automation. Prove it
works, build trust, and only then decide whether to expand into the full
audit, ontology-mapped engagement, and Company OS.

In ontology terms (Doc 06): a Pilot is a minimal **Engagement** scoped to
exactly one **Finding** and one **Automation Opportunity** — it skips the
full **Audit** across every Vertical. If the client is ready afterward, the
Pilot upgrades in place into a full Engagement rather than starting over.

## Why start this small

A first-time prospect who's never worked with you has no reason to sit
through a multi-vertical audit. A pilot removes that barrier: it's small
enough to say yes to on a single call, cheap enough to be low-risk for them,
and fast enough (1-2 weeks) that you can show a real result before asking for
anything bigger. Every quick win in Doc 04 Phase 1 is a candidate pilot;
inquiry/lead-response speed is the strongest default because it's visible,
easy to measure, and touches revenue directly.

## Step 1 — Pick the one business

Use Doc 01's prioritization: start with a wedding/event venue or a
winery/brewery/distillery sourced from the Dripping Springs Chamber
directory. Within that shortlist, prefer a business where, on the discovery
call, you can confirm:
- Inquiries arrive faster than the owner/team can respond to them (nights,
  weekends, multiple channels).
- There's no existing automation on this workflow (a real gap, not already
  solved).
- One person (often the owner) can say yes without a committee.

## Step 2 — Pick the one workflow: inquiry/lead response speed

This is the Sales vertical's "lead intake" Process (Doc 05 §1, Doc 06 core
ontology). It's the default pilot workflow because:
- It's the #1 quick win in Doc 04 Phase 1.
- Every business in the target verticals has it, in some form (phone, web
  form, email, social DM, walk-in).
- Slow response time is directly visible to the owner as "leads we lost,"
  making the ROI story concrete without needing their financial data.

## Step 3 — Scope the one automation

Keep the build itself minimal — this is not a CRM rollout:

- **Trigger:** a new inquiry arrives on whichever channel(s) the business
  actually uses (web form submission, missed call/voicemail, email to a
  shared inbox, or a social DM/comment).
- **Action:** send an immediate, personalized acknowledgment (text and/or
  email) confirming receipt and setting expectations ("we'll follow up within
  X hours"), and notify/route the inquiry to the right person so it isn't
  sitting unseen in a shared inbox.
- **Logging:** capture the inquiry in one simple, shared place (a
  spreadsheet is fine for the pilot) so nothing depends on memory — this
  becomes the first Data Object (Doc 06) and the seed of a future CRM if the
  engagement expands.
- **Explicitly out of scope for the pilot:** full CRM migration, lead
  scoring, multi-step nurture sequences, quote/proposal automation — those
  are Doc 04 Phase 2/3 and only come after the client is ready.

## Worked example (illustrative)

A representative Dripping Springs event venue/winery, sized like the
businesses identified in Doc 01: inquiries come in through a website contact
form, a shared Gmail inbox, and Instagram DMs. The owner checks these a
few times a day; anything that lands Friday evening often isn't answered
until Monday.

- **Before:** average first response time ~18-30 hours; the owner suspects
  (but can't prove) that some couples book with a faster-responding
  competitor venue instead.
- **Pilot automation:** web form and email inquiries trigger an instant
  auto-reply confirming receipt and giving a specific "you'll hear from us
  within 4 business hours" commitment, plus a text/Slack notification to the
  owner's phone; Instagram DMs get a saved-reply prompt so a human can send a
  fast, on-brand response instead of drafting from scratch. All three
  channels log to one shared tracking sheet.
- **After (target):** first response time under 4 hours across all
  channels; every inquiry visible in one place instead of split across three
  inboxes.
- **Timeline:** buildable in 3-5 days using the business's existing
  website/email/Instagram accounts — no new core system required.

## Step 4 — The Pilot One-Pager (what you hand the client)

Keep this to a single page:

1. **The problem** — in the owner's own words from the discovery call
   (e.g., "inquiries sit unanswered over the weekend").
2. **The one workflow** — inquiry response, named plainly.
3. **The one automation** — what will trigger, what will happen, what tools
   are touched (existing ones — nothing new to log into daily).
4. **Before/after metric** — current response time (estimated if not
   measured) vs. target response time.
5. **Timeline and cost** — 1-2 weeks, scoped as a fixed, small price (not a
   retainer yet).
6. **What happens next** — one line noting that if this works, there's a
   fuller review available (the full Process Audit, Doc 03) — but no pressure
   to commit to it now.

## Step 5 — Readiness signals to expand beyond the pilot

After the pilot runs for 1-2 weeks, look for these signals before proposing
anything bigger:

- The client actively references the before/after metric unprompted (e.g.,
  "we haven't missed a weekend inquiry since").
- The client asks a question that reaches into another Vertical (e.g., "can
  it also chase down unpaid invoices?" → Accounting & Finance).
- The client agrees to a follow-up call without you having to chase them.
- The client shares more operational detail than they did on the first call
  (a sign of trust, and a precondition for a real Audit).

If those signals show up, move to the full engagement: run the Process Audit
(Doc 03) across the applicable Verticals (Doc 05), then build the client's
Company OS (Doc 02's "Delivering the COS to clients") and sequence further
automation using the full roadmap (Doc 04). In ontology terms, the pilot's
single Finding/Automation Opportunity becomes the first row in a full Audit,
not a restart.

If the signals aren't there yet, don't push — log it, stay in touch, and
revisit in a quarter. A pilot that doesn't convert immediately is still a
case study for the next prospect in the same vertical.

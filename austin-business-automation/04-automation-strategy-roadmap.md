# Automation & Improvement Strategy Roadmap

Turns Process Audit findings (Doc 03) into a phased implementation plan.
Grounded in 2026 small-business AI adoption data: service triage, revenue
workflows (lead scoring, follow-up), and finance routines (invoicing,
collections) are the highest-ROI categories, and 82% of small-business
employers report having already invested in AI tools — speed of response is
now the main competitive differentiator for local service businesses.

## Phasing

### Phase 1 — Quick wins (first 30 days)
Low-effort, high-visibility automations that build trust and fund the next
phase:
- AI-assisted inquiry response (auto-acknowledge + route new leads within
  minutes instead of hours/days).
- Automated appointment/tour/tasting reminders (SMS + email) to cut no-shows.
- Automated review requests after service/event completion.

### Phase 2 — Core systems (30-90 days)
Bring the core revenue and finance workflows onto a connected system rather
than point fixes:
- CRM with AI-assisted lead scoring and follow-up sequencing (most businesses
  stop following up after 2 touches when 5+ are typically needed — this is
  where the most revenue leaks).
- Self-serve online scheduling/booking replacing manual back-and-forth.
- Automated invoicing and payment-reminder sequences (typical result pattern
  seen in comparable small businesses: AR follow-up time drops from hours/week
  to near zero and average days-to-payment roughly halves).
- Centralized reporting dashboard so the owner can see pipeline, bookings, and
  cash position without manually assembling it.

### Phase 3 — Advanced / agentic automation (6-12 months)
Once the client has adopted Phase 1-2 and trusts the systems:
- Conversational AI agents handling full scheduling workflows (propose times,
  book, confirm, reschedule, follow up on no-shows) end-to-end.
- AI-assisted proposal/quote generation for venues and professional services.
- Automated document/e-signature workflows for contracts and intake forms.
- Predictive/seasonal marketing automation (e.g., wedding-venue booking-season
  campaigns, winery event promotions) driven by CRM data.

## Vertical-specific plays

**Wedding & event venues (Camp Lucy, Duchman Winery, Bell Springs, and
similar):** inquiry-to-tour booking automation; automated quote/proposal
generation by package/date; day-of-event coordination checklists shared with
vendors; automated post-event review requests; seasonal drip campaigns for
peak booking windows.

**Wineries, breweries, distilleries, tasting rooms:** online
reservation/waitlist automation; POS and inventory sync so staff aren't
reconciling by hand; loyalty and email/SMS marketing automation (e.g., club
renewal reminders); systematic review-request automation tied to the tasting
trail's review-driven discovery model.

**Law firms & professional services (estate planning, real estate law):**
intake-form and conflict-check automation; e-signature and document-assembly
workflows; automated billing and trust-accounting reminders; client-portal
status updates to cut down on "what's my case status" calls.

**Real estate brokerages/agents:** lead routing and nurture sequencing across
channels; automated showing scheduling; transaction-coordination checklists
with status automation.

**Home services & contractors:** lead-capture-to-quote automation; dispatch
and crew scheduling; automated invoicing and follow-up; systematic review
generation (a top driver of local SEO for this category).

**Medical/dental/wellness practices:** appointment reminders and no-show
reduction; digital intake forms; automated insurance/billing follow-up.

## Tooling categories (not vendor-specific)

- AI voice/chat agents for intake and scheduling
- CRM platforms with built-in AI lead scoring and sequencing
- Workflow/integration automation (connects booking, CRM, invoicing, and
  communication tools without custom code)
- Reputation/review-management platforms with automated request + AI-assisted
  response
- AR/invoicing automation layered onto existing accounting software

Select specific tools per client based on their existing stack rather than
standardizing on one vendor — the goal is to reduce manual work with minimal
disruption, not to force a platform migration.

## Measuring success

Track the same metrics used to prioritize the audit (hours saved,
days-to-payment, no-show rate, response time, review volume/rating) before
and after each phase, and feed the results back into the consultancy's own
scorecard (`02-company-operating-system.md`) as client-facing proof points.
